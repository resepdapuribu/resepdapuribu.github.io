Nasi Goreng

Nasi Goreng is a popular Indonesian dish made of stir-fried rice, typically mixed with seasonings, vegetables, and protein such as chicken, beef, shrimp, or egg. It's a staple food in Indonesian cuisine and is considered the national dish of the country. It's quick, easy, and versatile, making it a popular choice for a quick meal or snack. Nasi Goreng is often served with a variety of condiments and garnishes, including sliced cucumber, crispy fried shallots, and a sweet soy sauce called kecap manis.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked rice
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 large tomato, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup of frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons of kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • Fresh chopped scallions and cucumber, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and garlic, cook until fragrant and softened.
  3. Add the diced tomato and cook until it's soft.
  4. Add the diced carrots and frozen green peas, cook for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  5. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and add the beaten eggs to the other side. Scramble the eggs until cooked.
  6. Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir to combine with the vegetables and eggs.
  7. Add the kecap manis, soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Stir until the rice is evenly coated.
  8. Serve with fresh chopped scallions and cucumber on the side.

Notes:

  • You can use any vegetables you like in this dish. Try adding sliced mushrooms, diced bell peppers, or shredded cabbage for variation.
  • Use day-old rice for the best texture, as fresh rice can get too sticky when stir-fried.
  • Kecap manis is a sweet soy sauce commonly used in Indonesian cooking. If you can't find it, you can substitute with regular soy sauce and add 1 tablespoon of brown sugar.
